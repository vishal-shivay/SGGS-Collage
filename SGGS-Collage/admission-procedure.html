Step-by-Step

Procedure for Admission

  • 1Every student desirous of seeking admission must procure a copy of the College Prospectus and admission form from the College office on payment of Rs. 400/- only.
  • 2Admission shall be given after the application form along with: (i) Photo copies of Detailed Marks Card, (ii) Category Certificate (SC/BC) and Punjab Domicile Certificate, and (iii) Character Certificate — are scrutinized and recommended by the admission committee and faculty in-charges.
  • 3Students seeking admission shall have to appear in person along with his/her parent or guardian before the Principal.
  • 4The decision of the Principal regarding approval/refusal of admission shall be final and binding on all concerned.
  • 5If a student fails to deposit dues immediately after approval, admission shall be treated as cancelled notwithstanding any provision in the college prospectus.
At the Time of Admission

Documents Required

  • 1Matriculation Certificate for verification of Date of Birth.
  • 2Character Certificate from the Head of the Institution last attended (in case of new student).
  • 3Attested copies of Certificates / Detailed Marks Card / Provisional Certificate of previous exam passed.
  • 4Migration Certificate from the university, if a candidate is migrating from another university last attended.
  • 5Copy of Registration Card (for those who are already registered).
  • 6Two copies of the latest passport size photographs to be pasted firmly at the place provided for in the admission form and identity-cum-library card.
  • 7Equivalent/Eligibility certificate in case of a student who wishes to join a course on the basis of qualification obtained from University/Board other than Panjab University/P.S.E.B. Mohali.
  • 8Category Certificate viz. SC / ST / BC / Blind / Physically Handicapped.
Regulations

Rules for Admission

  • 1If a student has a gap period of one year or more, he/she shall furnish an affidavit duly attested by the 1st Class Magistrate as proof of activities during the gap period.
  • 2A candidate joining the college for the first time must submit a character certificate from the head of the institution last attended.
  • 3Students whose results are R.L. or C.S. will be admitted provisionally for a period of three months on payment of all dues. Regularization is required once the result is declared.
  • 4Students who have passed their previous examination from another University/Board will be admitted at their own risk, subject to the approval by Panjab University.
  • 5In case of migration from another University, the student must submit the Migration Certificate within one month from the date of admission.
  • 6A student failing in all subjects or twice in the same class shall not be admitted.
  • 7Failed students of other institutions will not be admitted to any class.
  • 8A candidate who has been expelled or rusticated from anywhere shall not be admitted to the college.
  • 9Any candidate seeking admission on wrong information (age, subjects, percentage) — admission shall be cancelled and disciplinary action taken.
  • 10All admission to college shall be strictly provisional and subject to the rules/regulations of Panjab University, Chandigarh, which is the final authority.
  • 11Only students of Panjab University/P.S.E.B. Mohali can seek admission within ten days of declaration of their results, subject to availability of seats.
  • 12A student can be refused admission if his/her conduct during the previous year in the college has been unsatisfactory.
  • 13The Principal reserves the right to refuse/terminate any admission without assigning any reason.
Special Category

Compartment Candidates

  • 1A student of any other University placed in compartment in 1st Year/2nd Year of B.A./B.Sc./B.Com./BCA Course shall not be allowed to join the 2nd Year/3rd Year Course of Panjab University.
  • 2A candidate placed in compartment in +2 examination shall be eligible for 1st Semester admission provided: (i) compartment is in one subject only; (ii) at least 20% marks in that subject; (iii) requisite aggregate percentage; and (iv) compartment must be cleared before declaration of first year result.
  • 3The candidate admitted in 1st/3rd/5th semester will be allowed promotion to 2nd/4th/6th semester. However, for promotion from 2nd to 3rd or 4th to 5th semester, the candidate will have to clear 50% of the total papers till that semester. For calculating 50%, fractions like 2.5, 3.5, or 4.5 will be considered as 3, 4, or 5, respectively.
Financial

Fee and Other Charges

At the time of admission, six months full fee and other charges will be collected from all students. A fine of Rs. 5/- per day is charged for delay in payment. Absence from the college will not be accepted as an excuse for non-payment of fees on the due date. The names of students who do not pay fees by the due date shall be struck off from the college rolls. Such students may be readmitted on payment of Rs. 165/- as readmission fee along with late fee fine with the permission of the Principal.

Fee Notes

  • ✦Fees will be charged for 12 months in an academic year.
  • ✦Various college dues are subject to change with or without notice.
  • ✦Application for withdrawal must be signed by Parent/Guardian and presented to Principal after clearing dues.
  • ✦If a student doesn't get name removed from rolls, they will be treated on rolls for the purpose of realization of dues.

Fee Concession

  • ✦Applications must be submitted on prescribed form on or before dates to be notified.
  • ✦Concession/stipends are liable to be withdrawn if a student is found negligent in studies, remains irregular, or is guilty of misconduct.
  • ✦Agitational approach or participation in demonstrations against college authorities also results in withdrawal.
Change of Subjects/Faculty: A student joining 1st Semester may apply for change of subject/medium/option/faculty within one month of the commencement of the session or within one month from date of joining, whichever is later — as per University rules, subject to availability of seats. Application must be made on prescribed form available with the college office.
Academic Regulations

Lectures, Leave & Attendance Rules

Counting of Lectures

  • ✦Lectures shall be counted from the date of the first lecture delivered.
  • ✦Even if all students fail to turn up, they shall be marked absent and the lecture counted toward the total.
  • ✦Lecture shortage, if any, shall not be condoned.

Rules for Taking Leave

  • ✦Leave must be got sanctioned personally or by guardians/parents before it is availed of.
  • ✦Application for leave must be countersigned by parents or guardians.
  • ✦In case of illness, the application should be accompanied by medical certificate issued by a Registered Medical Practitioner.
Concessional Bus Passes: Students who require concessional bus passes are advised to contact the office superintendent.
Examinations

Mid-Semester Test

To prepare students for university examinations, the College conducts a mid-semester test in each semester in the months of October and March during the academic session. Monthly class tests may be conducted on any day at the discretion of the class teacher.

  • 1It is compulsory for students to appear in mid-semester test as well as class tests.
  • 2Exemption on account of serious illness or cogent reason is to be secured from the Principal before the commencement of such examination. Application must be supported by medical certificate/affidavit bearing the signatures of parent/guardian. No application entertained after the examination is over.
  • 3Absence from an examination for whatever reason means nil result and will invite heavy penalty. It shall also render the student ineligible for the University examination.
  • 4The Principal may at his discretion hold a special test for those who fail to fulfil conditions prescribed by the University.
University Examinations

Eligibility & M.Sc. Admission

A student shall be eligible to appear in the University examination only if he/she has attended the requisite number of lectures/practicals delivered to his/her class in each subject offered by him/her and obtained the required percentage of marks in the mid-semester tests conducted by the college as per University norms.

Admission to M.Sc. Part-1: In the subjects of Chemistry and Physics, admission will be on the basis of Entrance Test conducted by Panjab University Chandigarh (OCET). A candidate must pass the entrance test by securing the required percentage of marks and must meet other requirements laid down by the concerned university.
