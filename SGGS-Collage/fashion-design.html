Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible for B.Sc. Fashion Design?
Students who have passed 10+2 in any stream are eligible. Arts and Home Science backgrounds are advantageous but not mandatory. Aptitude for design and creativity is valued.
Is sewing or stitching knowledge required to join?
No prior stitching experience is required. All technical skills including pattern making, draping and garment construction are taught from the basics during the program.
Are fashion shows or exhibitions part of the course?
Yes — students participate in annual fashion shows, design exhibitions and portfolio presentations as part of their practical assessment and professional development.
Can I start my own fashion label after B.Sc.?
Absolutely. The curriculum includes entrepreneurship, fashion business and brand development modules that equip students to launch their own design label or studio.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the eligibility for M.Sc. Fashion Design?
A B.Sc. in Fashion Design or a related field (Home Science, Textile Design, Applied Arts) with minimum 45% marks. Candidates with a BFA may also be considered. Check with the admissions office for current criteria.
Is a dissertation/thesis compulsory?
Yes — all M.Sc. Fashion Design students complete an original research dissertation and a final collection project presented at a grand fashion show in Semester IV.
Will I learn CAD and digital design tools?
Yes — the program includes dedicated modules on Fashion CAD software, digital illustration and computer-aided pattern making to prepare students for the modern industry.
Are scholarships available for M.Sc. students?
Yes — merit and need-based scholarships are available under government and college-level schemes. Contact the admissions office for details on eligibility and application process.
