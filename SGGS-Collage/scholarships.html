Available Scholarship Schemes

The college facilitates multiple government and trust-funded scholarship programmes to ensure no deserving student is deprived of higher education due to financial constraints.

Principal Harbhajan Singh Memorial Trust

Every year 15 needy / meritorious students are awarded scholarships of Rs. 4,000/- each by the Principal Harbhajan Singh Memorial Trust, instituted by his family in the memory of the founder Principal of the college.

Post-Matric Scholarship – Scheduled Castes

Provides financial assistance to Scheduled Caste students at post-matric or post-secondary stage to enable them to complete their education. Applicable where parents'/guardians' annual income does not exceed Rs. 2,50,000/-.

Post-Matric Scholarship – Other Backward Classes (OBCs)

Assists OBC students at post-matric or post-secondary stage. Eligible for both employed students (family income ≤ Rs. 1 lac) and unemployed students whose parents'/guardians' income does not exceed Rs. 1 lac per annum.

Post-Matric Scholarship – Minority Communities

Awards scholarships to meritorious students from economically weaker sections of Muslim, Christian, Sikh, Buddhist, and Parsi communities (Class XI to Ph.D.) who secured ≥ 50% marks and have family income ≤ Rs. 2 lac.

Special Grant for Girl Students – Scheduled Castes

Girl students belonging to Scheduled Castes whose parents'/guardians' annual income does not exceed Rs. 60,965/- are entitled to a special grant of Rs. 50/- (Post-Matric) and Rs. 60/- per month (Post-Graduate).

Panjab University Scholarships

Awarded to students of affiliated colleges through the office of the Dean, College Development Council, Panjab University. Details available at dcdc.puchd.ac.in.

  • Means cum Merit
  • Sports
  • Single Girl Child
  • Physically Disabled
  • Aids / Cancer Patient

Sunny Oberoi Scholarships Scheme

Granted by Sarbat Da Bhala Charitable Trust (SDBCT) — a philanthropic body dedicated to extending economic benefits to underprivileged sections of society.

Eligibility

  • Students belonging to economically weaker sections
  • A student who has lost his/her father (earning member of the family)
  • Students whose mother is a divorcee not receiving any supporting allowance
  • Student whose family is affected by a natural disaster
  • Students who are differently abled
  • Students recommended by Managing Trustee of SDBCT (Regd.)

Rules

  • Parents' annual income must be less than Rs. 1 lac
  • Student must fulfill mandatory class attendance conditions
  • Student must not be availing SC/BC or Sikh Minority community scholarship
  • Student must have more than 65% marks in qualifying examination

📝 Important Note:

Scholarship/Stipend forms can be collected at the time of admission. Students eligible for scholarship/stipend must submit these forms along with their admission form, duly completed in all respects and attested by the appropriate authorities.

Forms not submitted by the due date notified by the college will not be entertained.

Students belonging to SC / OBC / Minority Communities must apply online for the scholarship before the due date.
